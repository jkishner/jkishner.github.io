Search Queries in Timers and Logbook in Due App Using Nested Lists in Launch Center Pro#

Tweet This Post#

Inspired by [this LCP action](http://launchcenterpro.com/p9zl3j) by [Alex Guyot](http://unapologetic.io/), I have written a Launch Center Pro action to search queries in either the Timers or Logbook section of Due: you can install it [here](http://launchcenterpro.com/dcs1xx).#

When you launch the action, you're presented with a choice between Timers or Logbook:#

 #

![](https://dl.dropbox.com/s/wv84z6lesoqmuhs/Photo%20Mar%2025%2C%202%2053%2014%20PM.png)#

 #

Then, depending on your choice, you're presented with a list of queries:#

 #

![](https://dl.dropbox.com/s/igrvmtqchoh330v/Photo%20Mar%2025%2C%202%2053%2017%20PM.png)#

![](https://dl.dropbox.com/s/sdnlg2v0a93ybwm/Photo%20Mar%2025%2C%202%2053%2021%20PM.png)#

 #

(Obviously, you will have to adapt for your own use. Yolanda cuts my hair.)#

After you choose the query, Due opens with the search already completed. You can then start a timer or recycle an old reminder.#

##A Breakdown of the Action#

 #

Here is the full string:#

 #

#

 #

Here I will URL-decode the first list item (between `Timers=` and the `|`):#

launchpro://?url=due%3A%2F%2Fx-callback-url%2Fsearch%3Fsection%3DTimers%26query%3D[list:Timers|Coffee|White rice|Collards|Freebird|Cauliflower|Chicken] #

The second list (the queries) is written as you would ordinarily compose a list in LCP. The important thing to note is that, since the Due URL scheme is not included in _those_ brackets, it has to be URL-encoded after the `launchpro://?url=`:#

due://x-callback-url/search?section=Timers&query=#
